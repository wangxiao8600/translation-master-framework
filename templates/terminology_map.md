## 4. Boundary Notes

For each important term, record:

- what the term must preserve
- what the term must not imply
- whether literal translation is acceptable
- whether target-language adaptation is allowed
- whether the term carries cultural or emotional force

| Term | Must preserve | Must not imply | Adaptation allowed | Human review |

## 5. Target-Language Fit

For each target term, check:

- Does it read naturally to native readers?
- Does it preserve the source work's conceptual boundary?
- Does it fit the sentence rhythm?
- Does it flatten emotional force?
- Does it over-localize the source idea?
- Does it under-localize a culturally loaded expression?

## 6. Consistency Review

Check the term across:

- title
- headings
- repeated phrases
- body text
- captions or notes
- glossary
- metadata

Review questions:

- Is the term stable where it should be stable?
- Is variation intentional where variation appears?
- Are related terms clearly separated?
- Are false friends or near-synonyms avoided?
- Are high-risk terms reviewed by a human?

## 8. Acceptance Checks

The terminology map is ready for the next stage when:

- core terms are identified
- target terms are reviewed for native-language fit
- meaning boundaries are written down
- high-risk terms are marked for human review
- known drift risks are documented
- final terms are consistent across the target text
- unresolved terms remain clearly marked
